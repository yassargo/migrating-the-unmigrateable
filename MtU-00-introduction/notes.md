📚 **Migrating the Unmigrateable - Study Notes | [MtU #00]** A learning-in-public series on legacy ICS migration and CRA compliance

**There's a ticking clock in industrial facilities across Europe. And most people responsible for them are hoping someone else figures it out.**

December 2027.

That's when the EU Cyber Resilience Act takes full effect.

Manufacturers of industrial control systems: PLCs, SCADA systems, HMIs ... etc  are already preparing. They'll ship CRA-compliant products. They'll update their datasheets. They'll be fine and cool as cucumber for the most part. This does not mean that they don't have any challenges, but their challenges are manageable once they follow the guidelines provided by CRA documentation.

But here's the problem nobody is talking about:

**The devices already installed aren't going anywhere.**

A PLC installed in 2009 (dare to say even before 2009) was designed to run for 25 years. It has no patch mechanism. No authentication. No encryption. The protocol it speaks was designed when physical isolation _was_ the security model, because nobody imagined these systems would ever touch a network connected to the outside world. It reminded me of an old commercial (I am not going to name the product, but you should know it by doing a simple search): "**It takes licking and keeps on ticking**" yes, ICS devices known for longevity and perseverance and I think it is one of the best engineered devices ever made 🦾 



Now they do. And in December 2027, the regulatory and insurance environment around them changes permanently.

The manufacturers are ready. The regulators are ready.

**The asset owners are not.** 😬⏳

---

I'm not a compliance lawyer. I'm not a 20-year OT veteran either.

What I am is someone who spent years in IT support, application management, vulnerability patching and cybersecurity monitoring, and when I started looking seriously at the OT and ICS world, one thing hit me immediately:

**Everything the IT world learned the hard way about security over the last 20 years , OT is about to learn all at once, under regulatory pressure, with zero downtime tolerance.**


I couldn't find a single practical, honest resource explaining step by step how you actually migrate a legacy ICS environment without destroying your uptime, your budget, or your sanity.

So I'm building that resource. In public. Post by post.

Over the coming months I'll cover:

- How to assess what you actually have _(spoiler: most plants don't fully know)_
- How to phase a migration without touching your 99.9% uptime requirement
- What CRA actually means for asset owners, not just manufacturers
- The templates and frameworks that make projects like this manageable

I'll cite sources. I'll flag what I know versus what I'm still figuring out. No fake authority — just honest research made useful.

If this is a problem you're living, managing, or consulting around, then follow along.
